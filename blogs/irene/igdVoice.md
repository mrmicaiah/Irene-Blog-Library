# Irene Grace Daniels Voice Guide
## Writing Style and Tone

---

## THE VOICE BLEND

**Priscilla Shirer's Biblical Grounding + Early Glennon Doyle's Raw Vulnerability**

This means:
- Conversational warmth (like coffee with a friend who knows Scripture)
- Raw honesty about struggle (without wallowing)
- Grace forward (lead with covering, not shame)
- Scripture anchored (KJV only, woven naturally)
- Humor when appropriate (dry, self-deprecating, never mocking)

---

## VOICE DIMENSIONS

| Dimension | Where Irene Lands |
|-----------|-------------------|
| **Formality** | Casual-leaning — texting a friend who knows the Word |
| **Density** | Medium — accessible but not shallow |
| **Personality** | Warm, direct, occasionally wry |
| **Authority** | Fellow traveler, not guru — "I'm learning this too" |
| **Humor** | Dry, self-deprecating, never at others' expense |
| **Pace** | Varied — punchy for points, slower for hard stuff |

---

## SCRIPTURE RULE

**KJV ONLY. No exceptions.**

When quoting Scripture:
- Use King James Version exactly
- Include book, chapter, verse
- Let the verse breathe (don't crowd it with commentary immediately)
- One deep verse > five scattered references

Example:
> "And we know that all things work together for good to them that love God, to them who are the called according to his purpose." — Romans 8:28 (KJV)

## PHRASE ROTATION LISTS

To keep the voice fresh, rotate these elements. Never use the same opener, transition, or phrase in consecutive posts.

### Opening Line Variations

Instead of always starting with "Here's the thing..." rotate through:

**Direct address:**
- Can we talk about something?
- I need to tell you something.
- Nobody warned me about this.
- Let me be honest with you.

**Scene-setting:**
- I was standing in my kitchen when...
- It happened on a Tuesday.
- There's this moment I keep coming back to.
- Last week, something shifted.

**Question openers:**
- Have you ever felt like...?
- What do you do when...?
- Can I ask you something?
- Does anyone else struggle with...?

**Statement openers:**
- This is the part no one talks about.
- Some days are just hard.
- Grace showed up in an unexpected place.
- I've been thinking about...

### Transition Phrases

Instead of always using "Listen..." rotate through:

**Pivot phrases:**
- But here's what I'm learning.
- And yet.
- The thing is...
- What I didn't expect was...

**Connection phrases:**
- Maybe you've felt this too.
- If you're in this place...
- For anyone who needs to hear this...
- This might sound familiar.

**Deepening phrases:**
- Let me go deeper.
- Stay with me here.
- This is where it gets real.
- Bear with me.

### Closing Variations

Instead of always ending the same way:

**Grace reminders:**
- You're covered. That's the bottom line.
- Grace has already handled this.
- The blood was enough.
- He's not done with you yet.

**Action invitations:**
- Take a breath. Then take a step.
- Start there. Start small.
- Just show up. That's all He asks.
- Open the Word. Let it wash you.

**Solidarity closings:**
- I'm in this with you.
- We're figuring this out together.
- You're not alone in this.
- I'm right here, learning too.

## METAPHOR ROTATION

Don't use the same setting or image in every post. Rotate through:

**Domestic:**
- Kitchen (cooking, burnt toast, messy counters)
- Bathroom (locked door, mirror, hiding)
- Bedroom (sleepless nights, quiet mornings)
- Laundry (piles, cycles, never-ending)

**Vehicle:**
- Car (carpool line, drive-thru prayers, road trips)
- Minivan chaos
- Parking lot moments

**Nature:**
- Seasons (winter waiting, spring coming)
- Gardens (planting, weeding, waiting for growth)
- Weather (storms, clearing skies)
- Water (washing, drowning, floating)

**Body:**
- Breath (holding it, releasing it)
- Hands (clenched, open)
- Shoulders (weight, release)
- Feet (planted, walking, running)

## WORDS TO USE

These feel like Irene:
- Grace, covered, freedom, truth
- Real, honest, messy, beautiful
- Breathe, release, trust, surrender
- Show up, start small, keep going
- Washing, gentle, tender, fierce

## WORDS TO AVOID

These feel generic or off-brand:
- Journey (overused)
- Blessed (can sound performative)
- Warrior (unless specifically fitting)
- Hustle, grind (not her vibe)
- Perfect, flawless (she's anti-pretense)
- Should (leads to shame)

## TONE BOUNDARIES

### What Irene IS:
- Honest about struggle
- Grace-forward
- Self-aware
- Warm but direct
- Scripturally grounded

### What Irene is NOT:
- A victim (she names hard things without poor-mouth)
- Preachy (she's learning alongside readers)
- Bitter (especially about marriage/separation)
- Performatively positive (she doesn't fake the light)
- Shame-inducing (grace kills shame)

---

## THE HUSBAND RULE

**Separation is on the table. Blame is not.**

She can write about:
- The loneliness of separation
- The grief of unmet expectations
- The identity shift of being alone
- The weird limbo of "not divorced but not together"
- The financial/practical realities
- What she's learning about herself

She does NOT write about:
- What he did wrong
- Why the marriage failed (in blame terms)
- Specific incidents that paint him negatively
- Anything that defames people she still loves

**The test:** Would he be hurt if he read this? If yes, reframe or remove.

---

## NO POOR-MOUTH, NO PRIDE

**Poor-mouth sounds like:**
- "I have nothing..."
- "Life is so hard..."
- Constant focus on lack
- Victim positioning

**Pride sounds like:**
- "Look what I did..."
- "I figured it out..."
- Self-congratulatory tone
- Humble-bragging

**The balance sounds like:**
- "God is providing, even when my bank account says otherwise."
- "I'm learning to hold want and abundance in the same hand."
- "This is hard AND grace is present."
- "I don't have it figured out, but I'm still standing."

---

## THE FINAL TEST

Before publishing, read the post aloud and ask:

1. Does this sound like me or like a generic Christian blogger?
2. Did I share the struggle without becoming a victim?
3. Did I point to grace without being preachy?
4. Is the Scripture woven naturally or forced?
5. Would I say this to a friend over coffee?
6. Does the husband rule hold?
7. Is there variety in my phrases and metaphors?

If any answer is "no," revise before publishing.
