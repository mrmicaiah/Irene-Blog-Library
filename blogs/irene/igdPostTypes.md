# Irene Grace Daniels Post Types
## Templates and Interview Questions

---

## OVERVIEW

Eight post types organized by length. Each includes structure template and interview questions to ask BEFORE drafting.

---

## SHORT POSTS (600-1,000 words)

### Type 1: THE REAL TALK
**Pillar:** The Real

**Use when:** You need to name something hard, call out a lie, or say what everyone is thinking but not saying.

**Structure:**
1. Hook (50-100 words) - Name the gap between expectation and reality
2. The Truth (200-300 words) - What it actually looks like
3. The Grace (200-300 words) - Where God meets you in it
4. The Takeaway (50-100 words) - One thing to hold onto

**Interview Questions:**
1. What expectation or lie are you calling out?
2. When did you first realize this was not what you expected? What was the moment?
3. What did you feel - not what you should have felt - but the raw emotion?
4. Where has grace shown up in this?
5. Is there a KJV scripture anchoring you right now?

---

### Type 2: THE WORD DROP
**Pillar:** The Word

**Use when:** A scripture hit you this week and you need to share it.

**Structure:**
1. The Scene (50-100 words) - Where you were, what was happening
2. The Verse (50-100 words) - The KJV scripture, given room to breathe
3. The Meaning (300-400 words) - What it meant to you
4. The Invitation (100-150 words) - What it might mean to them

**Interview Questions:**
1. What is the scripture that hit you? (KJV)
2. Where were you when it landed? Set the scene.
3. What was going on in your life that made this verse hit different?
4. What did God show you through it?
5. How can this apply to someone else?

---

### Type 3: THE LIGHT MOMENT
**Pillar:** The Light

**Use when:** Something funny happened, God showed up unexpectedly, or you need to celebrate.

**Structure:**
1. The Setup (100-150 words) - What was happening
2. The Turn (200-300 words) - Where it got funny or surprising
3. The Point (200-300 words) - What you learned (optional)
4. The Smile (50-100 words) - Light closing

**Interview Questions:**
1. What happened? Tell me the story.
2. What was the moment that made you laugh or surprised you?
3. Did this reveal something deeper, or is it just a good story?
4. How did this remind you of God's goodness?
5. Any scripture that connects? (Optional)

---

### Type 4: THE BEHIND-THE-CURTAIN
**Pillar:** The Behind

**Use when:** Sharing author life - writing struggles, book updates, creative process.

**Structure:**
1. The Update (100-150 words) - What is happening in the writing life
2. The Struggle or Joy (200-300 words) - The real behind the scenes
3. The Connection (200-300 words) - How readers can relate
4. The Invitation (50-100 words) - What is next

**Interview Questions:**
1. What is happening in your writing life right now?
2. What is the biggest struggle or breakthrough lately?
3. What are you learning about yourself through this?
4. How might a non-writer relate to this?
5. Any scripture speaking to your creative process?

## LONG POSTS (1,500-2,500 words)

### Type 5: THE GRACE ANCHOR
**Pillar:** The Grace

**Use when:** Unpacking a theological truth about grace, identity, or freedom.

**Structure:**
1. The Struggle (200-300 words) - The question or burden
2. What the World Says (200-300 words) - The wrong answer
3. What Scripture Says (400-500 words) - KJV passages
4. How Grace Changes It (400-500 words) - The transformation
5. The Application (200-300 words) - How to walk in this truth

**Interview Questions:**
1. What struggle or burden are you addressing?
2. What does the world say about this?
3. What KJV scriptures speak to this?
4. How does grace change the equation?
5. What would it look like to walk in this truth daily?
6. What is your personal experience with this?

---

### Type 6: THE EXPECTATION VS. REALITY
**Pillar:** The Real + The Grace

**Use when:** Addressing a specific gap - marriage, motherhood, faith, finances.

**Structure:**
1. The Expectation (300-400 words) - What you thought it would be
2. The Reality (400-500 words) - What it actually is
3. The Grief (200-300 words) - Naming the loss
4. The Grace (400-500 words) - How God meets you
5. The Invitation (200-300 words) - What to do with this

**Interview Questions:**
1. What expectation are we talking about? Where did it come from?
2. When did reality hit? What was the moment?
3. What did you grieve? What did you lose?
4. Where has grace shown up?
5. What KJV scripture is anchoring you?
6. What would you say to someone just entering this reality?

---

### Type 7: THE TEACHING POST
**Pillar:** The Word or The Grace

**Use when:** You have a concept or principle to unpack.

**Structure:**
1. The Hook (150-200 words) - Why this matters
2. The Problem (300-400 words) - What is broken or misunderstood
3. The Principle (500-700 words) - The teaching with Scripture
4. The Application (300-400 words) - How to live it out
5. The Challenge (150-200 words) - One action step

**Interview Questions:**
1. What principle do you want to teach?
2. Why does this matter? What problem does it solve?
3. What KJV scriptures support this?
4. How have you applied this in your own life?
5. What is one practical step someone could take?
6. What misunderstanding are you correcting?

---

### Type 8: THE STORY
**Pillar:** Any

**Use when:** A personal story carries the weight of the post.

**Structure:**
1. Scene Setting (200-300 words) - Time, place, context
2. The Tension (400-500 words) - What was at stake
3. The Turn (400-500 words) - The shift or revelation
4. The Resolution (300-400 words) - Or sitting in unresolved
5. The Takeaway (150-200 words) - What it means for them

**Interview Questions:**
1. What is the story? Give me the broad strokes.
2. Set the scene - where, when, who?
3. What was the tension? What was at stake?
4. What was the turning point?
5. How did it resolve? (Unresolved is okay too)
6. What does this story teach?
7. Is there KJV scripture woven in?

---

## POST TYPE SELECTION GUIDE

| If you want to... | Use this type |
|-------------------|---------------|
| Name something hard | The Real Talk |
| Share a scripture moment | The Word Drop |
| Share something funny | The Light Moment |
| Update on writing life | The Behind-the-Curtain |
| Unpack a grace truth | The Grace Anchor |
| Address an expectation gap | The Expectation vs. Reality |
| Teach a concept | The Teaching Post |
| Tell a story | The Story |
