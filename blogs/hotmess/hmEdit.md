## 1. NARRATIVE REVIEW

### Check These Elements

| Element | Question | Fix If Needed |
|---------|----------|---------------|
| **Opening Hook** | Does the first paragraph pull readers in? | Strengthen the opening image or line |
| **Through-Line** | Is there a clear thread from start to finish? | Identify the spine and reinforce it |
| **Scene Grounding** | Can readers see, hear, smell where they are? | Add 1-2 sensory details per scene |
| **Emotional Arc** | Does the feeling shift from beginning to end? | Identify the turn and make it land |
| **Payoff** | Is the ending earned? Does it resonate? | Adjust setup if payoff feels unearned |
| **Standalone** | Could a new reader understand this without context? | Add minimal context if needed |

### Red Flags
- ГўВќЕ’ Story meanders without direction
- ГўВќЕ’ The "lesson" is stated outright instead of shown
- ГўВќЕ’ Ending doesn't connect to beginning
- ГўВќЕ’ Reader needs previous stories to understand

## 2. VOICE REVIEW

### The Balance Check

| Element | Target | Problem Signs |
|---------|--------|---------------|
| **Lawson (chaos)** | ~40-50% | Too manic, loses meaning |
| **Backman (heart)** | ~40-50% | Too earnest, loses energy |
| **Unique Rena** | ~10% | Missing coffee/faith elements |

### Voice Markers Present?

- [ ] At least one tangent or spiral (Lawson)
- [ ] At least one short, punchy emotional beat (Backman)
- [ ] Self-aware commentary
- [ ] Over-explanation when nervous or excited
- [ ] Specific vocabulary used casually

### Voice Red Flags
- ГўВќЕ’ All chaos, no heart
- ГўВќЕ’ All heart, no humor
- ГўВќЕ’ Generic narrator voice (could be anyone)
- ГўВќЕ’ Preachy tone
- ГўВќЕ’ Self-pity instead of self-deprecation

## 5. CLUMSINESS CHECK

### Must Be Present
Every story should have at least one clumsiness moment:
- [ ] Knocking something over
- [ ] Standing in a spill without noticing
- [ ] Tripping or bumping
- [ ] Coffee grounds somewhere unexpected
- [ ] Physical comedy paired with competent coffee-making

### Placement
- [ ] Does it happen naturally, not forced?
- [ ] Does it serve the story (comedy, tension, or character)?
- [ ] Is it acknowledged without being belabored?

### Tone
- [ ] Played for warmth, not pity
- [ ] Self-aware but not self-flagellating
- [ ] Has consequences but they lead somewhere

### Red Flags
- ГўВќЕ’ No clumsiness at all (missing her watermark)
- ГўВќЕ’ Clumsiness feels forced or inserted
- ГўВќЕ’ Played for cringe instead of charm
- ГўВќЕ’ She's suddenly graceful for no reason

## 7. SCRIPTURE INTEGRATION

### Check For
- [ ] Theme verse/principle present (subtle or direct)
- [ ] Not preachyГўв‚¬вЂќwoven in naturally
- [ ] Matches the story's theme from `hmAxes.md`
- [ ] Feels like Rena's internal framework, not sermon

### Integration Levels

| Level | Description | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **Direct Quote** | Rena quotes the verse | RarelyГўв‚¬вЂќmajor moments only |
| **Paraphrase** | Rena thinks the concept in her words | Often |
| **Echo** | The verse's truth is lived, not stated | Most common |
| **Absence** | No scripture, theme still present | Occasionally okay |

### Red Flags
- ГўВќЕ’ Verse feels inserted, not organic
- ГўВќЕ’ Preachy explanation of what the verse means
- ГўВќЕ’ Romans 8:1 when it's not a grace story
- ГўВќЕ’ No connection to the stated theme

### Special Note on Romans 8:1
This is her anchor verse. Use it when:
- She's fighting condemnation/guilt specifically
- The theme is Grace
- It's a major emotional moment

Do NOT use it:
- Every story
- When another verse fits better
- Just because it's "her verse"

## 8. FOOTNOTE COUNT

### The Rule
**Maximum 3 footnotes per story. Often fewer. Sometimes none.**

### Check
- [ ] Count: _____ footnotes
- [ ] Each one earns its place
- [ ] None break the story's flow
- [ ] They add, not distract

### If Over 3
Cut the weakest. Ask:
- Which footnote is least essential?
- Could this information be in the main text?
- Does cutting it hurt anything?

### Footnote Quality Check
- [ ] Adds humor or insight
- [ ] Sounds like Rena's internal voice
- [ ] Brief (1-2 sentences max)
- [ ] Doesn't explain what should be shown

---

## 9. FINAL READ

### The Out-Loud Test
Read the entire story aloud (or mouth the words). Check:
- [ ] Dialogue sounds natural spoken
- [ ] Rena's voice is consistent throughout
- [ ] Pacing feels right when performed
- [ ] Emotional beats land

### The Stranger Test
Could someone who's never read the blog:
- [ ] Understand the story?
- [ ] Care about Rena?
- [ ] Feel the emotional payoff?
- [ ] Want to read more?

### The Fan Test
Would a loyal reader:
- [ ] Recognize this as Rena's world?
- [ ] See something new or different?
- [ ] Feel satisfied by the ending?
- [ ] Notice the growth/continuity?

## TITLE GENERATION

After story passes edit review:

### Generate 3-5 Titles
Format: "In Which [Something Happens] and [Something Else Results]"

**Good Titles:**
- Hint at the story without spoiling
- Capture the Lawson tone
- Are specific to THIS story
- Make readers curious

**Examples:**
- "In Which I Learn That Silence Isn't Always Empty"
- "In Which I Ruin a Marriage Proposal and Accidentally Save One Instead"
- "In Which I Discover That Perfect Isn't on the Menu"

### Present Options

2. "In Which..."

4. "In Which..."
5. "In Which..."
