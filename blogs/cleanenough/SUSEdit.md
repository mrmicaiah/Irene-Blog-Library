# Clean Enough House - Editorial Review Process

## Purpose

The edit phase catches issues before title generation. Reviews differ between Anchor and Lighter posts — they have different jobs and different success criteria.

---

## Anchor Post Review

Anchor posts (Tuesdays) are substantial, structured, and usually teach something. Review them against these criteria.

### 1. VOICE

Check against SUSVoice.md. Specifically:

- **Late-30s Emily, not frazzled-young-mom Emily.** A panicky tone reads as off-character.
- **Specific names, numbers, scenarios** throughout.
- **Honest about failure** without wallowing.
- **Permission-giving** rather than prescriptive.
- **Short paragraphs.** No text walls.

Red flags:
- Generic mom-blog cheeriness ("Hey friends!")
- Too formal or too distant
- Vague scenarios without details
- Generic "kids" or "husband" instead of names
- Performative struggle

---

### 2. OPENING

Check whether the post starts in the middle of action with specifics. Strong openings name a time, a person, a situation.

**Strong:**
"Lucas texted at 4:47 PM. I had thirty minutes."

**Weak:**
"Today I want to talk about organization."

If the opening is weak, the fix is usually rewriting the first 50 words to drop the reader into a specific moment.

---

### 3. TEACHING (When Present)

For Anchor posts that teach a method:

- Is the method clearly named and connected to SUSFramework.md?
- Are steps specific and ordered?
- Does the post explain *why*, not just *what*?
- Are common pitfalls warned about?
- Is there an actionable takeaway?

If teaching is present but unclear, the fix is usually adding numbered steps and a "why this works" paragraph.

---

### 4. STORY-TO-TEACHING BALANCE

Different formats have different balances. Refer to SUSPostTypes.md for the target ratio.

- **Disaster Chronicle:** ~40-50% story, ~50-60% teaching
- **Method Tutorial:** ~20-30% story, ~70-80% teaching
- **Zone Transformation:** ~50-60% process, ~40-50% teaching/products
- **Product Roundup:** Story is light, products are the spine
- **Quick Win:** Mostly tactic, minimal story
- **Myth Buster:** ~30% setup, ~40% reality, ~30% alternative
- **System Breakdown:** Mostly teaching, story is ballast

If the balance is off, the fix is trimming or expanding the appropriate section.

---

### 5. AFFILIATE INTEGRATION

If products appear (max 2 — global rule):

- Is the affiliate disclaimer present at the start of the product section?
- Does each product have specific use case from Emily's life?
- Are there honest pros AND cons?
- Is price mentioned?

Red flags:
- Missing disclaimer
- More than 2 products in the post
- Generic praise without specificity
- No cons listed

---

### 6. CHARACTERS

Check that everyone in the post acts consistently with SUSCharacters.md. The Bible is the source of truth.

If a character does something inconsistent with the Bible, the fix is to either revise the action or — if the inconsistency reveals something real about how the character has evolved — note it for a separate Bible update conversation. Don't quietly drift the character.

---

### 7. STRUCTURE

Verify the post follows the chosen format from SUSPostTypes.md:

- All required sections present
- Section lengths appropriate
- Flow between sections works

---

### 8. ARC OR THREAD TOUCHES

Does this post touch an arc or thread (see SUSArcs.md)?

If yes:
- Does the touch advance the arc, or just nod to it?
- If it advances, does it move at the right pace? (No premature climaxes.)
- After publication, the arc's "where we are" line needs updating.

If no, that's also fine. Not every post moves something.

## Lighter Post Review

Lighter posts (Thursdays) are shorter, looser, and don't always teach. Review them against different criteria.

### 1. VOICE

Same as Anchor: late-30s Emily, specific, honest. **One adjustment:** Lighter posts can run quieter. A Field Notes that ends in a shrug isn't a problem. A Letter that's tender without being funny isn't a problem.

The one constant: it has to *sound like Emily*. A Lighter post that drifts into generic essay voice or social-media voice has lost the thing that makes it part of this blog.

---

### 2. THE MOMENT

The most important Lighter review question: **does this post have a real moment in it?**

A Lighter post that's just observations without a specific scene, or a Q&A with a vague invented question, or a List with generic items — those have no anchor. The reader can't picture anything.

The fix is to find or invent a *specific* anchor — a real time, a real scene, a real detail — and rebuild around it.

---

### 3. FORMAT INTEGRITY

Each Lighter format has a job (see SUSPostTypes.md). Check whether the post is doing its format's job:

- **Field Notes** — is there a real scene? Specific time, place, sensory detail?
- **Q&A** — is the question concrete? Is the answer how Emily would actually answer a friend?
- **List** — are the items specific (real things, real moments)? Does the form do the work?
- **Status Report** — is the arc/thread well enough established that this beat lands? Or is this premature?
- **Observation** — is there a real idea, or is it just a feeling dressed up as one?
- **Letter** — does the letter framing hold? Or does it slip into essay halfway through?
- **Photo Walk** — are there real photos of actual current state?

If the format isn't doing its job, the fix is either to deepen into the format or to switch to a different one. (A "Field Notes" with no scene is probably actually an Observation.)

---

### 4. LENGTH

Lighter posts target 600-900 words. Some formats run shorter (List, Observation: 500-800).

If a Lighter is over 1,000 words, it's drifting toward Anchor territory. The question is whether to cut it back or to recognize it as an accidental Anchor and reslot. (If the latter: this is rare and worth thinking carefully about — Anchor slots are valuable.)

If a Lighter is under 500 words, it's probably underdone. Either expand or cut.

---

### 5. AFFILIATE POSTURE

Lighter posts should default to **no products**. If a product appears, it should be:

- Organic to the moment (not structured around the product)
- One product, not two
- Linked once, not repeatedly

If a Lighter has structured product recommendations, it's actually a Roundup misfiled. Move it to Anchor.

---

### 6. CHARACTERS

Same as Anchor: everyone acts consistently with SUSCharacters.md.

---

### 7. ARC OR THREAD TOUCHES

Lighter posts are *especially* good for thread touches — a phone call to Dad, a Marie drop-by, a Sherry text. These keep the world dense without requiring a full arc beat.

If a Lighter post could organically touch a thread and doesn't, it's thinner than it needed to be. The fix is usually a single line that adds texture without changing the post's shape.

## Edit Feedback Format

Use this structure for both Anchor and Lighter reviews:

```
---
EDIT REVIEW — [ANCHOR | LIGHTER]

**STRENGTHS:**
- [Specific things working well]

**VOICE NOTES:**
- [Issues or confirmations]

**MOMENT / TEACHING:** (whichever applies)
- [Whether the moment lands / whether teaching is clear]

**STRUCTURE NOTES:**
- [Format integrity, balance, length]

**CHARACTERS:**
- [Any inconsistencies with the Bible]

**ARCS/THREADS:**
- [What's touched, what could be touched]

**AFFILIATE CHECK:** (if products present)
- [Disclaimer, count, integration quality]

**RECOMMENDED CHANGES:**
1. [Specific fix #1]
2. [Specific fix #2]

**OVERALL:** [Ready for titles / Needs revision / Major issues]

Ready for title options? (Say 'titles' to proceed)
---
```

## Common Issues and Fixes

### Opening is weak/generic
**Fix:** Rewrite first 50 words to start with a specific moment — time, name, scene.

### Voice too formal
**Fix:** Add contractions, shorten sentences, inject dry humor, use family names.

### Voice too frazzled
**Fix:** Cut exclamations, slow the pacing, add a quietly observed detail. Emily knows the patterns; let her sound like she does.

### Teaching unclear (Anchor)
**Fix:** Add numbered steps, explain "why," give a concrete example.

### No clear moment (Lighter)
**Fix:** Find or invent a specific anchor scene. Rebuild around it.

### Products feel forced
**Fix:** Connect each product to a specific scenario from Emily's life. Add what didn't work.

### Format drift
**Fix:** Check format job in SUSPostTypes.md. Either deepen into the format or switch.

### No arc/thread touch
**Fix:** Add a single organic line — a phone call, a name, a passing reference.

---

## When to Recommend Major Revision

For Anchor posts:
- Opening fundamentally weak with no salvageable specifics
- Voice completely off
- Teaching unclear or disconnected from framework
- Story-teaching ratio severely off
- Multiple structural problems

For Lighter posts:
- No real moment, no real idea, no real scene — pure abstraction
- Format drift so severe it's a different format entirely
- Voice slipped into something that isn't Emily

For major revision:
- Don't just list problems
- Suggest a specific direction
- Identify what can be salvaged
- Give a clear path forward

---

## When Post Is Ready for Titles

**Green lights:**
- Voice is consistently late-30s Emily
- Opening is strong and specific
- Format is doing its job
- Characters are consistent
- Arcs/threads touched where appropriate
- Length matches slot
- Affiliate posture matches slot (none for Lighter unless organic; max 2 for Anchor)

Minor wording tweaks are fine to defer. Real structural problems get addressed before titles.
